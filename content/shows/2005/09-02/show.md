summary: 'In the wake of one of the largest natural disasters in U.S, history, New Orleans is scrambling to provide essentials for its survivors. The larger task of reconstruction and remediation looms, and analysts say Katrina''s long-term effects will last for decades. Living on Earth''s Jeff Young talks with Hugh Kaufman, a senior policy analyst for the EPA, who says the city''s construction and lack of environmental enforcement made the storm''s damage worse than it could have been. Meanwhile, workers at Louisiana''s Department of Environmental Quality are surveying the area for chemical contamination, and guest host Bruce Gellerman talks with DEQ''s Chris Roberie about their findings. Also, a page from history: New Orleans Times-Picayune reporter Mark Schleifstein remembers the aftermath of Hurricane Betsy in 1965, and a lesson on how not to rebuild.'
